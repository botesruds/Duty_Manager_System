# Walkthrough video scripts

Three short videos. Record each one separately and share with the relevant audience.
Read the script in normal conversational pace — the bracketed `[...]` lines are stage
directions for what to do on screen, not things to say out loud.

Tools: [Loom](https://www.loom.com) (free, easiest), or macOS Screen Recording
(`⌘ + ⇧ + 5`). Sign in to the app in an incognito window beforehand using a real test
account so nothing personal is visible.

---

## Video 1 — For teachers (~2 min)

**Audience**: every teacher who books duties.
**Setup**: open the app in an incognito window. Pre-create one teacher account that
already has a couple of bookings + an assigned location. Also have the booking
window open and the schedule published, so confirmation states are visible.

### Script

> Hi everyone, this is a quick tour of the new duty manager. You'll use it to book
> your duties, see your schedule, and request swaps with other teachers.

[Open the login page]

> You log in with your **employee number** — same one HR uses — and a password.
> Everyone starts with the default password `Duties2026!`. On your first login the
> system will force you to change it before you go any further.

[Type emp_no, log in. Land on Dashboard]

> This is your **dashboard**. The two big numbers at the top show how many duties
> you still need to book — break duties on the left, lunch duties on the right. As
> you book, that number drops.

[Point at the "Your duties this week" section]

> Below, you'll see every duty you've already booked, grouped by day. Each card
> shows the duty type and either a **green Confirmed badge with your location** —
> meaning the admin has finalised your spot — or an amber **Pending** badge if
> they're still working on it.

[Click "Browse slots"]

> To book new duties, click **Browse slots**. The sticky bar at the top tracks
> your progress in real time. Below that, every slot is listed by day. Click
> **Book** to claim one. If you change your mind, you can cancel it while the
> booking window is open.

[Book one slot to demonstrate. Show counter ticking down]

[Click "Request a swap"]

> If you need to swap a duty, use **Request a swap**. Pick the duty you want to
> give up, choose your preferred new day — or "any other day" — and post the
> request. Other teachers will see it; if anyone has the matching duty and is
> willing to trade, they can accept it instantly.

[Click "Self report"]

> On the day of your duty, come here to **Self report**. You'll see today's duty
> and your location. Tap **Mark present** when you're at your spot. That's all
> you need to do.

> Any questions, come find me. Thanks!

## Video 2 — For monitors (~1.5 min)

**Audience**: staff designated as monitors (they confirm other teachers' attendance).
**Setup**: log in as a monitor account in incognito. Make sure the schedule is
published and there are teachers booked for today so the list isn't empty.

### Script

> Hi — this video is for our duty monitors. As a monitor, you don't book duties
> yourself; your job is to confirm that other teachers turned up to their posts.

[Open login page, type emp_no, log in]

> Sign in with your employee number and password just like the teachers do.

[Lands on Check-in page]

> You land straight on the **Check-in** page. That's the only page you need.

> Today's duty list is grouped into **Break**, **Lunch A**, and **Lunch B**.
> Each row shows the teacher's name, where they should be standing, and two
> status columns.

[Point at "Self" column]

> The **Self** column shows whether the teacher has already self-reported. Green
> tick means they did; "Not yet" means they haven't.

[Point at "Monitor confirm" column]

> The **Monitor confirm** column is your job. When you've checked someone is
> actually on duty at their spot, tap **Confirm** in their row. The button
> becomes a confirmation showing your name and the time.

[Tap Confirm on one row to demonstrate]

> The list syncs live — if another monitor confirms someone from their phone,
> you'll see it appear here automatically.

> That's it. Walk the building, confirm everyone, you're done.

## Video 3 — For admins (~3 min)

**Audience**: people running the system day-to-day.
**Setup**: log in as admin in incognito. Have some sample data — a few teachers,
a few locations, a few bookings — so each page has something to show.

### Script

> This is the admin overview. As admin you set up the system, manage staff and
> locations, build the weekly schedule, and review attendance.

[Land on Overview page]

> **Overview** shows headline numbers and the master toggle for the booking
> window. When this is **Open**, teachers can book. When you close it, they
> can't book or cancel — usually you close it once you have everyone's
> selections and you're ready to build the schedule.

[Click "Staff"]

> **Staff** lists everyone. You can **Upload CSV** for bulk loads at the start
> of the year, or **Add staff** to enter one person at a time. Each row has a
> role pill — click it to toggle a teacher to a monitor. There's also a
> **Reset password** button — that puts them back to the default `Duties2026!`
> — and a **Delete** button if someone leaves the school.

[Click "Departments"]

> **Departments** is where you set default duty quotas. New staff inherit their
> department's defaults; you can override per-person on the Staff CSV or form.

[Click "Locations"]

> **Locations** is your physical list of duty spots. You tag each as
> **indoor** or **outdoor** — we use the indoor set in summer, the outdoor set
> the rest of the year. Click any location name to rename it. Add new ones
> with the form.

[Click "Bookings"]

> **Bookings** lets you manually assign or remove duties — useful for handling
> exceptions. Most of the time you won't touch this; teachers self-book.

[Click "Schedule"]

> **Schedule** is where the real work happens once bookings are in. Pick a
> day tab. Up top is the **Unassigned strip** with everyone booked for that
> day. Below are the **location cards**.

[Drag a name from Unassigned into a card]

> Drag a teacher's name into any location card to assign them. Drag them out
> again to un-assign. The progress bar tracks how many you've placed. When
> you're done for the week, click **Publish schedule** — teachers will then
> see their assigned locations.

[Switch season demo, if relevant]

> The **Switch to indoor / outdoor** button at the top flips between
> location sets when the seasons change.

[Click "Master"]

> **Master** is a read-only view of the entire week — all duties, all
> locations, all teachers. Click **Download CSV** to export it for printing
> or sharing.

[Click "Attendance"]

> **Attendance** is the daily report. Pick a date and you'll see who was
> marked present, who self-reported, and which monitor confirmed them.

[Click "Check-in" — same page monitors use]

> The **Check-in** tab is the same one your monitors see. As admin you have
> access too, in case you need to confirm attendance directly.

> That's the whole tour. A typical week is: open booking window → teachers
> book → close booking window → drag-and-drop the schedule → publish →
> monitors confirm attendance → review the report.

## After recording

- Watch each video back in full before sharing — it's much easier to spot a
  stumble or a wrong click than to notice it while recording.
- Trim any long pauses at the start or end (Loom does this automatically in
  most cases).
- Send links via your usual school comms channel. Keep the videos somewhere
  you can re-record easily, because the platform will keep evolving and the
  videos will date.
